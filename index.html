About

About the book

Compared with typical calculus textbooks, Calculus Fennicus is more ambitious: a unified, logically progressing introduction — starting from the very foundations — to the basic ideas of mathematics and to modern computational technique. It is intended for a demanding audience for whom mathematics is not only technique and formulas but an intellectual challenge.

A distinctive feature: sequences and series are introduced right at the outset, so the real numbers can be defined as they appear in the world of computers — as infinite decimals.

The book grew out of the advanced first-year course Pitkäranta lectured at Helsinki University of Technology from 2000 to 2013 — for some of the most mathematically demanding students in the country, who had come up through the Finnish school system in the very years it topped the international PISA rankings. It was written to satisfy that audience: readers for whom a formula is not yet an answer until they can see why it is true.

That may matter more, not less, in the age of AI. When machines handle the calculation, the lasting advantage is in understanding why the mathematics works — and this book builds every result, in order, from the ground up.
